NEat is a smartphone app that promotes collaboration, unity and sharing, while leading the way to an eco-friendlier society, with no costs. The app is based on posting leftover food and sharing it with others. Maybe you managed to make a portion too much for dinner, or that you had a lot of extra food left after a celebration. Instead of throwing it away, you can post it on the app and share it with others. At the same time as a user, you will be notified if there are leftover food, fruit or vegetables in your local area. You can thus drop by and pick it up, or if they want, stay and eat with them.

How will it help society toward a greater direction?


The app will manly start up in Norway, before it is expanded to other countries and places. In Norway, more than 390,000 tonnes of edible food is thrown away every year. And the household sector alone accounts for more than half of this food waste; 58%. (https://www.matsentralen.no/matsvinn) By encouraging people to share the extra food they have left, instead of throwing it away, will lead to a big change and difference in numbers. Even if the action is small, the effect of it is great, both towards society and oneself.
